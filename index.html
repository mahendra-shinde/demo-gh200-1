The Solar System

A simple guide to the Sun and the eight planets

The Solar System is centered around the Sun, a star whose gravity keeps planets, moons, asteroids, and comets in orbit. The eight planets are divided into rocky inner planets and gas/ice giant outer planets.

Mercury

The smallest and closest planet to the Sun, with extreme temperature changes.

Venus

A hot planet with a thick atmosphere, often called Earth’s "sister planet."

Earth

The only known planet with life, featuring liquid water and a protective atmosphere.

Mars

The "Red Planet," known for its iron-rich surface and evidence of ancient water.

Jupiter

The largest planet, a gas giant with the Great Red Spot and many moons.

Saturn

Famous for its bright ring system, Saturn is a large gas giant.

Uranus

An ice giant that rotates on its side, giving it unusual seasons.

Neptune

The farthest planet from the Sun, known for strong winds and deep blue color.

Tip: Planet distances and sizes are not to scale in this summary.
